Math and physics provide the fundamental principles that electrical engineers apply to design, analyze, and optimize electrical systems. Math offers the tools (calculus, linear algebra, differential equations) to quantify and manipulate electrical concepts, while physics (especially electromagnetism) describes the underlying behaviors of electricity and other energy forms. For example, an engineer uses calculus to analyze changing voltages, the laws of physics to design circuits, and linear algebra to model complex electrical networks.

    Used for analyzing and solving complex electrical networks and systems.

Interplay in Practice

- **Problem-Solving:**
    
    Engineers use mathematical frameworks to apply physical principles to solve real-world electrical challenges. 
    
- **Circuit Analysis:**
    
    Engineers use mathematical tools like calculus and integral transforms to model and analyze complex circuits, which are built on fundamental physical laws. 
    
- **System Design:**
    
    Electrical engineers use mathematical models based on physics to design everything from simple wires to complex processors and power grids.
